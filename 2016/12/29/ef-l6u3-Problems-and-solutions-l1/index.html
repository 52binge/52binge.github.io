Problems and solutions L1 Explaining a problem   2016-12-29


1. Vocabulary

  • addicted
  • insomnia
  • marital
  • alcohol
  • gambling
  • cigarettes
  • stressful

John needs to make some changes.

A: Sarah - B: MARK
[MARK] Sarah, it’s like 115 degrees outside.
[Sarah] I ‘m addicted
[MARK] Are you serious?
[Sarah] Anyway, What’s your problem?
[MARK] Sarah, when you drink coffee, you smoke.
[Sarah] I stopped smoking.

2. problems

problems
They’re addicted to gambling.
He’s addicted to alcohol. He’s an alcoholic.
She has financial problems.
He has a weight problem. He’s overweight.
I have a problem sleeping. I have insomnia.
They have marital problems.

3. Expressions

sentence
She can’t stop. She’s addicted to cigarettes.
She has a lot of personal problems.
I’m sorry to hear that.
She’s having financial problems.
The trouble is that she’s addicted to shopping.
Oh, no. That’s terrible.
